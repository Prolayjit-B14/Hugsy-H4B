Understanding ADHD

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects attention regulation, impulse control, and activity levels.

People with ADHD often have different brain development and neural activity that affects executive functioning.

Key Challenges:

  • Maintaining focus on less stimulating tasks
  • Regulating attention and switching between activities
  • Planning, organizing, and managing time
  • Controlling impulses and managing emotions
  • Working memory and recall of instructions

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and approach.

If you notice your child struggling with learning, behavior, social skills, communication, or sensory processing more than their peers, it may indicate a need for evaluation. Warning signs include consistent academic difficulties despite support, unusual behavioral patterns, delayed developmental milestones, or challenges with friendships. Our initial consultations can help determine if a comprehensive evaluation would be beneficial.

Our evaluations are comprehensive and typically occur over 2-3 sessions. They include standardized testing, clinical observations, parent and teacher questionnaires, and a thorough developmental history. We assess cognitive abilities, academic skills, attention, executive functioning, social-emotional development, and any specific areas of concern. After completing all assessments, we provide a detailed report with diagnosis (if applicable) and personalized recommendations.

Most therapy sessions are 45-60 minutes long, depending on your child's age, attention span, and specific needs. The frequency of sessions varies based on the treatment plan, but typically range from once weekly to twice monthly. We regularly assess progress and adjust the therapy schedule accordingly, with the goal of gradually reducing frequency as your child develops skills and strategies.

We accept many major insurance plans, including Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Medicaid, and Tricare. Coverage varies depending on your specific plan and benefits. Our administrative team can verify your benefits before your first appointment and explain any potential out-of-pocket costs. We also offer flexible payment plans and a limited number of sliding scale spots for families without adequate insurance coverage.
